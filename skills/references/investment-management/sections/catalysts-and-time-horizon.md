# Catalysts And Time Horizon

- Catalysts connect thesis evidence to a plausible path for price realization,
  risk reduction, income realization, or portfolio role improvement.
- Catalysts can include earnings inflection, product launch, regulatory
  approval, capital return, debt refinancing, divestiture, index inclusion,
  management change, margin recovery, or industry cycle normalization.
- Time horizon should match the evidence: near-term event, medium-term
  fundamental improvement, long-duration compounding, income generation, or
  tactical exposure.
- Monitoring markers should be observable and tied to the thesis, such as
  revenue growth, margin progress, cash conversion, leverage reduction,
  customer metrics, regulatory milestones, or capital allocation behavior.
- Absence of a catalyst does not invalidate every thesis, but it changes the
  patience requirement and increases the importance of valuation support and
  opportunity cost.
- Thesis review should distinguish delayed evidence from broken evidence when
  timing slips.
