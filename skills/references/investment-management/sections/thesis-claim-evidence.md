# Thesis Claim Evidence

- An investment thesis should separate the claim, evidence, assumptions,
  valuation view, risk view, catalyst, and expected time horizon.
- Evidence quality depends on source reliability, recency, relevance,
  independence, data coverage, and whether the evidence directly supports the
  stated claim.
- Thesis evidence can include financial statements, segment data, industry
  data, management commentary, unit economics, competitive position,
  regulatory facts, customer behavior, valuation history, and scenario analysis.
- A source can support a factual observation without supporting the investment
  conclusion drawn from it.
- Assumptions should be visible where the thesis depends on growth, margins,
  capital intensity, discount rates, multiples, market share, pricing power, or
  management execution.
- Unsupported narrative smoothing occurs when uncertainty, conflicting evidence,
  or weak source fit is hidden behind a confident investment story.
