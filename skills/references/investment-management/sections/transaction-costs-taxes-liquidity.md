# Transaction Costs Taxes Liquidity

- Investment changes should be interpreted net of commissions, spreads, market
  impact, fund redemption fees, turnover costs, bid-ask depth, and expected
  slippage.
- Tax-sensitive review should account for lot selection, holding period,
  realized gains or losses, wash-sale constraints, tax-loss harvesting, income
  character, and account type.
- Liquidity constraints include trade size relative to volume, lockups, gates,
  settlement timing, private asset restrictions, short-sale availability, and
  market stress behavior.
- A theoretically useful rebalance can be unattractive when implementation
  frictions consume the expected risk or return benefit.
- Cost and tax estimates should be tied to a specific account, jurisdiction,
  instrument, and trade size when those details are available.
- If tax or liquidity evidence is unavailable, conclusions should separate the
  gross investment rationale from the unverified net implementation result.
