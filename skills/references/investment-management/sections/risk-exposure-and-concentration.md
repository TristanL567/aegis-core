# Risk Exposure And Concentration

- Portfolio risk is shaped by asset class, sector, issuer, geography, currency,
  duration, credit quality, factor exposure, leverage, liquidity, and
  correlation structure.
- Concentration can appear through direct position size, overlapping funds,
  common factors, common revenue drivers, shared counterparties, or hidden
  exposure through derivatives and structured products.
- Factor exposures such as equity beta, value, growth, momentum, quality, size,
  rates, credit, commodity, and currency risk can explain behavior that simple
  position weights obscure.
- Risk decomposition should distinguish standalone position risk from marginal
  contribution to portfolio risk.
- Correlations are unstable across regimes; diversification that looks strong in
  normal periods may weaken during stress.
- Exposure review should state whether the relevant concern is volatility,
  drawdown, tail loss, income stability, liquidity stress, tracking error, or
  mandate breach.
