# Valuation And Fundamentals

- Valuation evidence should state the method: multiples, discounted cash flow,
  sum-of-parts, asset value, liquidation value, dividend discount, replacement
  cost, or precedent transactions.
- Comparable valuation needs peer relevance across business mix, growth,
  margin, capital intensity, leverage, accounting policy, geography, and
  cyclicality.
- Fundamental quality includes revenue durability, margin structure, return on
  invested capital, balance sheet resilience, cash conversion, reinvestment
  opportunity, and governance.
- Growth assumptions should be checked against market size, share gains,
  pricing, volume, churn, capacity, regulation, and competitive response.
- Valuation can be optically cheap because earnings are temporarily high,
  assets are impaired, leverage is elevated, or business quality is declining.
- A valuation claim should distinguish current price, intrinsic value estimate,
  downside case, upside case, and margin of safety.
