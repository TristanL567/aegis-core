# Portfolio Policy And Mandate

- Portfolio review should be anchored to the account purpose, investment
  objective, target risk level, liquidity needs, time horizon, income needs,
  benchmark, and permitted instruments.
- Mandates can constrain position size, asset classes, leverage, derivatives,
  credit quality, duration, currency exposure, sector concentration, tax
  sensitivity, ESG restrictions, or cash minimums.
- A position can be attractive in isolation and still be unsuitable when it
  conflicts with portfolio purpose, risk tolerance, liquidity needs, or policy
  limits.
- Policy ranges and strategic targets provide context for whether a change is
  discipline-preserving, opportunistic, or outside the agreed allocation
  framework.
- Mandate interpretation should distinguish hard constraints from preferences,
  temporary exceptions, and advisor discretion.
- When the mandate is missing or ambiguous, investment conclusions should state
  that suitability and rebalancing judgments are conditional on assumed
  objectives and constraints.
