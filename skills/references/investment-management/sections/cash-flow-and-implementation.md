# Cash Flow And Implementation

- Cash flows can reduce the need for selling: contributions, dividends,
  interest, maturities, distributions, and new deposits can fund rebalancing
  toward underweight targets.
- Withdrawals, spending needs, capital calls, tax payments, and reserve targets
  can make liquidity planning more important than exact target-weight
  restoration.
- Implementation choices include trade sequencing, minimum trade sizes,
  fractional-share availability, settlement cycles, fund cutoffs, market hours,
  and account-level restrictions.
- Rebalancing with cash flows may lower taxes and trading costs, but can delay
  risk correction when drift is large or market exposure is urgent.
- Cash buffers should reflect near-term liabilities, client preferences,
  expected volatility, and the cost of holding cash relative to policy targets.
- Implementation evidence should identify whether a recommendation assumes
  immediate trading, staged trading, cash-flow netting, or no-trade monitoring.
