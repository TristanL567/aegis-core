# Allocation Drift And Thresholds

- Allocation drift compares current weights with target weights, policy ranges,
  tolerance bands, and any tactical tilts currently authorized.
- Drift can come from market movement, cash flows, dividends, withdrawals,
  currency effects, corporate actions, or deliberate non-rebalance decisions.
- Rebalancing thresholds should balance risk control against transaction costs,
  tax impact, liquidity, and the value of letting low-risk drift self-correct.
- Threshold types include calendar schedules, absolute percentage bands,
  relative percentage bands, volatility-aware bands, risk-budget bands, and
  cash-flow-first rebalancing.
- A rebalance case is stronger when drift meaningfully changes risk exposure,
  violates policy, creates concentration, or moves the portfolio away from its
  intended objective.
- Small drift inside policy bands usually needs stronger evidence before
  justifying a trade, especially when costs or taxes are material.
