# Investment Management Reference

## Scope

Reference scope: Axis-2 knowledge for investment management review, including
portfolio mandate alignment, allocation drift, rebalancing thresholds,
implementation frictions, risk exposures, investment thesis evidence,
valuation context, counterevidence, catalysts, and time horizon boundaries.

It is not a procedural skill and is not independently executable.

## Consuming Skills

- `portfolio-rebalancing-review`: expected `reference_pointers` sections are
  `portfolio-policy-and-mandate`, `allocation-drift-and-thresholds`,
  `transaction-costs-taxes-liquidity`, `risk-exposure-and-concentration`, and
  `cash-flow-and-implementation`.
- `investment-thesis-evidence-check`: expected `reference_pointers` sections
  are `thesis-claim-evidence`, `valuation-and-fundamentals`,
  `risk-and-counterevidence`, `catalysts-and-time-horizon`, and
  `portfolio-policy-and-mandate`.

## Sections

| id | topic | open when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| portfolio-policy-and-mandate | Mandate, objectives, constraints, risk tolerance, and policy alignment. | Open when investment work depends on portfolio purpose, constraints, or mandate boundaries. |
| allocation-drift-and-thresholds | Target allocation, drift bands, rebalancing thresholds, and trade urgency. | Open when reviewing whether allocation drift supports or weakens a rebalance case. |
| transaction-costs-taxes-liquidity | Trading costs, tax lots, liquidity, spreads, and implementation frictions. | Open when investment action depends on net benefit after trading, tax, or liquidity effects. |
| risk-exposure-and-concentration | Factor, sector, issuer, currency, duration, leverage, and concentration exposure. | Open when portfolio review depends on risk decomposition or exposure concentration. |
| cash-flow-and-implementation | Contributions, withdrawals, cash buffers, order sequencing, and practical execution constraints. | Open when portfolio changes interact with cash needs or implementation mechanics. |
| thesis-claim-evidence | Investment claim structure, evidence quality, source reliability, and assumption support. | Open when checking whether an investment thesis is supported by cited evidence. |
| valuation-and-fundamentals | Valuation measures, fundamentals, growth, margins, balance sheet, and comparables. | Open when thesis evidence depends on valuation or fundamental business quality. |
| risk-and-counterevidence | Bear case, downside scenario, uncertainty, conflicting evidence, and evidence limits. | Open when thesis review needs risks, counterarguments, or unsupported-claim boundaries. |
| catalysts-and-time-horizon | Catalysts, expected timing, monitoring markers, and thesis horizon fit. | Open when thesis credibility depends on event timing, patience, or observable milestones. |

## Out Of Scope

- Activation rules, workflow steps, output contracts, or procedural behavior.
- Portfolio construction algorithms, trading recommendations, regulated advice,
  order execution systems, tax advice, or validator tooling.
- Role prompt content, procedure definitions, or new investment model-family
  skills.
